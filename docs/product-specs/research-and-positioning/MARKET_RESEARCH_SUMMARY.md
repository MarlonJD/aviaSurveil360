# Market Research Summary

## Main conclusion

The market already understands the surveillance process: audit planning, checklists, findings, corrective actions, evidence, reminders, dashboards and reports. The opportunity for AVIASURVEIL 360 is not to invent a new regulatory process. The opportunity is to make a known process easier to operate.

## What competitors and adjacent products show

EMPIC describes broad surveillance capabilities such as planning, checklist execution, findings/observations, CAP handling, evidence handling and external stakeholder access. Centrik and similar aviation QMS products emphasize documents, dashboards, safety, workflows, risk and compliance. iQSMS highlights internal/external audits, auditee guest access and due-date communication.

These signals confirm that the user's proposed structure is correct: **Surveillance Plan → Audit Checklist → Finding → CAP → Evidence → Review → Closure → Dashboard**.

## Common usability gaps seen in the market

Public regulator-specific reviews are limited, but adjacent aviation QMS/EHS/compliance reviews repeatedly point to these problems:

1. Too many modules shown to every user.
2. Complex navigation and high learning curve.
3. Powerful configuration that normal users do not understand.
4. Dashboard/reporting that requires expert knowledge.
5. Dense or dated UI.
6. Statuses that do not clearly show owner, due date and next action.
7. Auditee processes still happening by e-mail.
8. CAP acceptance and finding closure being confused.
9. Evidence scattered across e-mail and shared drives.
10. Management reporting assembled manually.

## AVIASURVEIL opportunity

Build a product that makes the core loop obvious:

- Plan audits.
- Run checklists.
- Issue findings.
- Receive CAPs.
- Review evidence.
- Close findings.
- See risk and overdue items.

## Design implication

The back office may be configurable, but the user interface must be role-based and simple. The product should not expose internal objects such as workflow engine, taxonomy, evidence repository and notification engine as primary navigation items for normal users.
