# Product Positioning and Key Decisions

## Recommended product name

**AVIASURVEIL 360**

Optional suite logic:

- AVIAAUTHORITY 360: suite
- AVIALICENSE 360: personnel licensing product
- AVIASURVEIL 360: surveillance and oversight product

## One-line pitch

AVIASURVEIL 360 is a task-based surveillance, audit, findings, CAP and evidence management platform for Civil Aviation Authorities.

## What it replaces

- Spreadsheet surveillance plans.
- Word/PDF audit reports with manual follow-up.
- E-mail-based CAP submission.
- Shared-drive evidence folders.
- Manual due-date reminders.
- Manually assembled management reports.

## Differentiation

AVIASURVEIL should not be positioned as a cheaper EMPIC copy. It should be positioned as a lighter, clearer surveillance workbench:

- Strong auditee portal.
- Simple finding lifecycle.
- Clear next action and owner.
- CAP acceptance separated from closure.
- Evidence review with version history.
- Dashboard that gives actions, not chart overload.
- Configurability hidden from normal users.

## Hard decisions

1. Make Auditee Portal a core module, not a later add-on.
2. Treat Finding as the central object.
3. Keep CAP and Evidence as separate objects.
4. Do not close findings when CAP is merely accepted.
5. Use Due Date / Target / Overdue language instead of heavy SLA language.
6. Keep risk-based planning and mobile/offline for later phases.
7. Use templates for aviation domains, not separate products per domain.
8. Link with AviaLicense through shared organizations and risk flags, not by merging products.
